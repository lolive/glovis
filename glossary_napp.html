Project Name
Name used to uniquely identify a project.
Valid codes include:
NAPP = CIR film
NAPPA = Duplicate B&W film, covering limited area
NAPPW = B&W film
Roll Number
The number assigned to a roll of film.
Frame Number
A number that indicates an image position on a roll of film.
Acquisition Date
This will reflect the first date of the collection period (8-day or 16-day) if a composite granule. Otherwise, will reflect the calendar date of acquisition for a daily granule.
NOTE: May not agree with "Range Beginning Date", which reflects the first date within a collection period that data meeting minimum quality standards was acquired.
Format:
YYYY/MM/DD
Film Type
Aerial film type used for aerial mapping.
Valid Codes:
B&W = Black-and-white
CIR = Color Infrared
Flight Line
Flight Line and Station numbers indicate the positions upon which NAPP photographs are centered. These positions are based on specific locations within the boundaries of associated 7.5-minute topographic map quads.
Basically, the flight line number consists of a longitude degree coordinate, the position of the associated 7.5-minute quad, and "E" or "W" which indicates whether the flight line is to the east or west of the center of the quad.
Format Example: Flight Line Number: 0998W
099 = Longitude degree coordinate
8 = Location of the quad in relation to the positions of the eight quads in a degree of longiitude
W = Indicates whether the flight line is to east or west of the center of the quad
Station
Station is a number relative to the flight line. It occupies a specific position within the 7.5-minute quad(s) ascending to the north beginning with the lowest latitude of the U.S., meaning the lowest numbers would be in Florida and the highest numbers would be in Maine.
Upper Left Corner
Product tile upper left corner latitude and longitude.
Format:
DDD°MM'SS"H
Where:
DDD = Degrees (Valid values: 24-49N for Latitude, 66-126W for Longitude)
MM = Minutes (Valid values: 0-59)
SS = Seconds (Valid values: 0-59)
H = Hemisphere (Valid values: N or S for Latitude, E or W for Longitude)

Scene Center
The latitude and longitude of the center point of the image. NAPP photographs are acquired over predetermined center points (center latitude and center longitude) which are defined by their location within a 7.5-minute quadrangle. The NAPP images are flown north to south or south to north.
Format:
DDD°MM'SS"H
Where:
DDD = Degrees (Valid values: 24-49N for Latitude, 66-126W for Longitude)
MM = Minutes (Valid values: 0-59)
SS = Seconds (Valid values: 0-59)
H = Hemisphere (Valid values: N or S for Latitude, E or W for Longitude)
County
The county in which the photograph was acquired.
State
This field is used to identify the state in which the photograph was taken.
Camera
The number assigned to an aerial photographic device composed of a lightproof enclosure having an aperture with a shuttered lens through which the image of an object is focused and recorded on a photosensitive film or plate.
Calibrated Focal Length
The distance measured along the lens axis from the rear nodal point to the imaging surface plane in millimeters.
Lens
The lens is generally composed of multiple lens elements that gather the light rays from a scene (frame) and bring them to focus in the focal plane.
Project Number
This field is used to identify the project number, which consists of the contract year and the sequential number of the contracted area.
Entity ID
This field is used to identify data by assigning a unique series of letters and numbers to an image.
Format
DDPPPPPPRRRRRFFF
Where:
DD = Dataset Identifier
PPPPPP = Project Name
RRRRR = Roll Number *
FFF = Frame Number *
Format Example:
NP0NAPP001060121
NP = Dataset Identifier
0NAPP0 = Project Name
01060 = Roll Number *
121 = Frame Number *
* Depending upon the entity ID, some of the information may be preceded by zero-filled spaces.
Product Available
The products available for a digital photo.
Valid Values:
Medium Res and Scanned = Medium resolution digitized products and High Resolution scanned products are available
Medium-resolution digitized files are created at approximately 600 dpi with an output image size of around five inches square. The file sizes are approximately 15 megabytes for black and white images and 45 megabytes for color images.
Scanned only = Only high-resolution scanned products are available
High-resolution scanned files are created at approximately 1200 dpi with an output image size of around nine inches square. The file sizes are approximately 120 megabytes for black and white images and 360 megabytes for color images.
