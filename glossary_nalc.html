Acquisition Date
Definition:Date data was acquired for each scene in decade.
Format: YYYY/MM/DD

Browse Availability
Definition:This flag identifies whether or not the entity has a browse image available with it. A browse image provides the user with a visual overview of an inventory item. This is an on-line digital (raster format) image of an individual scene.

Valid Codes:
Y = Yes
N = No

Cloud Cover
Definition: Cloud Cover is the percentage of the entire image obscured by clouds and their shadows. The percentage is displayed in increments of 10%.

Valid Codes:
0 = 0% but less than 10%
1 = 10% but less than 20%
2 = 20% but less than 30%
3 = 30% but less than 40%
4 = 40% but less than 50%
5 = 50% but less than 60%
6 = 60% but less than 70%
7 = 70% but less than 80%
8 = 80% but less than 90%
9 = 90% up to 100%

Entity ID
Definition: This field uniquely identifies data which can be ordered in any combination of unique products.
Example of NALC Entity ID: LPNALC046029U

Valid Format: DDNNNNPPPRRRX
D = Data set idenifier
N = NALC
P = Path
R = Row
X = R for restricted data , U for unrestricted data

Map Projection
Definition:Map Projection used on scenes.
Valid value is Universal Transverse Mercator (UTM)

Path
Definition: The Path is the longitudinal center line of a Landsat scene, corresponding to the center of an orbital track. Sequential numbers from east to west are assigned to 233 nominal satellite tracks for Landsat 4 and 5. Landsat 1-3 satellites have 251 tracks (paths). Path numbers can be used with Row numbers to designate a geographic search for a nominal scene center.

The consistency and repeatability of the path for each satellite provides a common reference for all scenes acquired. The path is also a key indexing field for subsequent acquisition requests as well as data base inquiries.


Processing Level
Definition: The processing level applied to the scene.
Valid codes:
C = Composite
E = DEM Extracted
G = Geocoded/Georegistered
T = Terrain Corrected/Georegistered

Resampling Technique
Definition: The resampling applied to the scene.
Valid code: CC = Cubic Convolution

Resampling Technique DEM
Definition: The resampling applied to the DEM.
Valid code: BI = Bilinear

Row
Definition: The Row is the latitudinal center line of a Landsat scene. This nominal configuration begins with row 1 at North 80 degrees 47 minutes and ends at row 122 at South 81 degrees 51 minutes for daylight acquisitions. Row 60 corresponds with the equator at 0 degrees latitude. A total of 248 rows are involved for all Landsat missions. Rows 123-248 are associated with night acquisitions.

Scene ID.
Definition: The scene id of the image used in the decade.

Upper Left Corner
Definition: Product tile upper left corner latitude and longitude.

Format: DDD°MM'SS"H
DDD = Degrees (Valid values: 0-90 for Latitude, 0-180 for Longitude)
MM = Minutes (Valid values: 0-59)
SS = Seconds (Valid values: 0-59)
H = Hemisphere (Valid values: N or S for Latitude, E or W for Longitude)

Upper Right Corner
Definition: Product tile upper right corner latitude and longitude.

Format: DDD°MM'SS"H
DDD = Degrees (Valid values: 0-90 for Latitude, 0-180 for Longitude)
MM = Minutes (Valid values: 0-59)
SS = Seconds (Valid values: 0-59)
H = Hemisphere (Valid values: N or S for Latitude, E or W for Longitude)

Lower Left Corner
Definition: Product tile lower left corner latitude and longitude.

Format: DDD°MM'SS"H
DDD = Degrees (Valid values: 0-90 for Latitude, 0-180 for Longitude)
MM = Minutes (Valid values: 0-59)
SS = Seconds (Valid values: 0-59)
H = Hemisphere (Valid values: N or S for Latitude, E or W for Longitude)

Lower Right Corner
Definition: Product tile lower right corner latitude and longitude.

Format: DDD°MM'SS"H
DDD = Degrees (Valid values: 0-90 for Latitude, 0-180 for Longitude)
MM = Minutes (Valid values: 0-59)
SS = Seconds (Valid values: 0-59)
H = Hemisphere (Valid values: N or S for Latitude, E or W for Longitude)

Scene Center
Definition: The actual latitude and longitude of the center point on the full scene. Hemisphere indicated by the N = North, S=South, E=East and W = West.

Format: DDD°MM'SS"H
DDD = Degrees (Valid values: 0-90 for Latitude, 0-180 for Longitude)
MM = Minutes (Valid values: 0-59)
SS = Seconds (Valid values: 0-59)
H = Hemisphere (Valid values: N or S for Latitude, E or W for Longitude)
