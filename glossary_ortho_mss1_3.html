    Entity ID
    Definition: This field uniquely identifies the scene for ordering from Earth Explorer.

     
    Format: EMPpppRrr_sTyyyymmdd
    ppp Path
    rr Row
    s Satellite number.
    3 = Landsat 3
    2 = Landsat 2
    1 = Landsat 1
    yyyy Year of acquisition
    mm Month of acquisition
    dd Day of acquisition
    Scene Size
    Definition: Size of the total granule in megabytes, including the .hdr and .jpg files.
    Acquisition Date
    Definition: The date the image was acquired.

     
    Format: YYYY/MM/DD
    Scene Cloud Cover
    Definition: The Scene Cloud Cover is the percentage of the entire image obscured by clouds and their shadows. The percentage is displayed in increments of 10%. Cloud Cover is a subjective call made from interpreting a single band of the image on film. It is therefore, approximate in nature.

    Note: a small number of scenes were categorized with inaccurate cloud cover information. Before being added to the data archive, all selected images had to be cloud-free or contain minimal cloud cover which resulted in a small number of scenes to be categorized as having a 90% cloud cover, which is inaccurate. The problem is currently being investigated.

    WRS-1 Path
    Definition: The WRS-1 Path number associated with the scene.

     
    Valid Values:
    1 through 251

    The Path is the WRS-1 path into which the scene falls. Sequential numbers from east to west are assigned to 251 nominal satellite tracks. Path numbers can be used with Row numbers to designate a geographic search for a nominal scene center.

    The consistency and repeatability of the path for each satellite provides a common reference for all scenes acquired.

    WRS-1 Row
    Definition: The WRS-1 Row number associated with the scene.

     
    Valid Values:
    1 through 248

    The Row is the WRS-1 row into which the scene falls. This nominal configuration begins with row 1 at North 80 degrees 1 minute and 12 seconds, and ends at row 119 at South 80 degrees 1 minute and 12 seconds for daylight acquisitions. Row 60 corresponds with the equator at 0 degrees latitude.

    Zone
    Definition: UTM zone number.
    Upper Left Corner
    Definition: Scene upper left corner latitude and longitude.

     
    Format: DDD°MM'SS"H
    DDD = Degrees (Valid values: 0-90 for Latitude, 0-180 for Longitude)
    MM = Minutes (Valid values: 0-59)
    SS = Seconds (Valid values: 0-59)
    H = Hemisphere (Valid values: N or S for Latitude, E or W for Longitude)

    Sun Azimuth
    Definition: Azimuth is the angle of horizontal deviation, measured clockwise, of a bearing from a standard direction.
    Valid Values:
    -180.000000 through +180.000000 degrees

    A positive value [+] indicates angles to the East or clockwise from North; a negative value [-] indicates angles to the West or counter-clockwise from North.

    Sun Elevation
    Definition: Elevation is the angle of vertical deviation, measured clockwise, of a bearing from a standard direction.
    Valid Values:
    -90.000000 through +90.000000 degrees

    A positive value [+] indicates a daytime scene; a negative value [-] indicates a nighttime scene.

    Day or Night
    Definition: This field indicates whether the scene was collected during the day or night.

     
    Valid Codes:
    Day
    Night
    Browse Available
    Definition: This field indicates whether a browse image is available.
