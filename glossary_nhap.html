Project Name
Name used to uniquely identify a project.
Valid codes include:
NHAP84 = NHAP followed by the year contracted
NAPP02 = NHAP second cycle
Roll Number
The number assigned to a roll of film.
Frame Number
A number that indicates an image position on a roll of film.
Acquisition Date
The acquisition date refers to the date the image was acquired.
Format:
YYYY/MM/DD
Film Type
Aerial film type used for aerial mapping.

Black-and-white (B&W) panchromatic film is sensitive to the full visible portion of the electromagnetic spectrum of light and captures images in tones of gray ranging from black to white.

Color infrared (CIR), or false color, film is sensitive to green and red in the visible portion of spectrum of light and extends into the near-infrared region of the spectrum. CIR photography utilizes high speed film that is subject to degradation prior to processing, which can create an overall blue cast to the image.
Valid Values:
Black and White
Color Infrared
Scale
The black-and-white film is exposed in a precision aerial camera with a focal length 15.3 cm (6 inches), producing a photograph at a scale of 1:80,000 (1 inch equals about 1.25 miles). Each black-and-white exposure covers nearly 130 square miles of terrain. The color infrared film is exposed in a second aerial camera with a focal length of 21.0 cm (8.25 inches), producing a photograph at a scale of 1:58,000 (1 inch equals about 0.9 miles). Each color infrared exposure covers nearly 68 square miles of terrain.
Upper Left Corner
Product tile upper left corner latitude and longitude.
Format:
DDD°MM'SS"H
Where:
DDD = Degrees (Valid values: 24-49N for Latitude, 66-126W for Longitude)
MM = Minutes (Valid values: 0-59)
SS = Seconds (Valid values: 0-59)
H = Hemisphere (Valid values: N or S for Latitude, E or W for Longitude)

County
The county in which the photograph was taken.
State
This field is used to identify the state in which the photograph was taken.
Nominal Focal Length
The distance measured along the lens axis from the rear nodal point to the imaging surface plane used for the classification of lenses.
Valid Values:
6 inches for B&W
8.25 inches for CIR
Entity ID
This field is used to identify data which can be ordered in any combination of unique products.
Format:
DDAPPPPPPRRRRFFF
DD = Data Set Identifier (NB for black & white; NC for color infrared)
A = Agency Identifier where 1 indicates USGS
PPPPPP = Project Name (6 characters)
RRRR = Roll Number (4 characters) *
FFF = Frame Number (3 characters) *
Format Example:
NC1NHAP840053084
NC = Color Infrared
1 = USGS Agency Identifier
NHAP84 = Project Name
0053 = Roll Number *
084 = Frame Number *
* Depending upon the entity ID, some of the information may be preceded by zero-filled spaces.
Product Available
The products available for a digital photo.
Valid Values:
Medium Resolution and Scanned Products = Medium resolution digitized products and High Resolution scanned products are available
Medium-resolution digitized files are created at approximately 600 dpi with an output image size of around five inches square. The file sizes are approximately 15 megabytes for black and white images and 45 megabytes for color images.
Scanned only = Only high-resolution scanned products are available
High-resolution scanned files are created at approximately 1200 dpi with an output image size of around nine inches square. The file sizes are approximately 120 megabytes for black and white images and 360 megabytes for color images.
